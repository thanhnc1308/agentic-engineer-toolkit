# Role Overview and Responsibilities (L3/L4)

Use this as the baseline expectation set before scoring competencies.

## Developer (L3)

### Overview

- Primary activity: creating and deploying software to meet business needs.
- Focus on product outcomes, not just shipping code.
- Use AI tools to accelerate development and spend more time on system understanding, assumption validation, and user value.

### Responsibilities

- Software Development:
- Build tested, maintainable software.
- Ensure commits include automated tests.
- Understand and work with CI/CD pipelines.
- Extend and work with existing codebases.
- Operations & Reliability:
- Implement logging, monitoring, and alerts.
- Focus on user-facing impact, not only system health.
- Consider second-order effects across systems.
- Product Thinking:
- Use user signals to guide decisions.
- Understand product context behind features.
- Raise concerns when requirements do not match user problems.
- Propose product/engineering tradeoffs.
- Seek early validation (prototypes, demos, feedback loops).
- Close the loop by measuring feature success.
- Delivery:
- Maintain high shipping velocity.
- Deliver at App/Service level.
- Understand dependencies across teams.
- Ownership & Initiative:
- Take ownership of delivered software.
- Proactively pick up bugs, improvements, and gaps.
- Raise tech debt with user-impact context.
- Manage priorities and progress.
- Collaboration:
- Work closely with Product Managers, Designers, and Data Analysts.
- Contribute to standups, backlog reviews, retrospectives.
- Demo work with user-impact framing.
- Mentorship:
- Occasionally mentor Graduate Developers and new hires.
- Ensure understanding of fundamentals behind AI-generated code.

### To Get To Senior Developer (L4)

- Continuously improve technical and product skills.
- Be approachable and help others learn.
- Actively mentor teammates.
- Work effectively with cross-functional stakeholders.
- Bring user evidence into discussions.
- Build trust across teams.
- Improve team effectiveness and developer experience.
- Enable others to be more productive.
- Share user insights.
- Navigate unclear problems confidently.
- Communicate changes clearly.
- Validate assumptions early.
- Consider second-order effects.
- Drive correct technical decisions.
- Frame work in terms of product outcomes.
- Close the loop on delivered features.
- Maintain exemplary code quality.
- Promote automation over repetitive work.
- Champion product quality and code quality.
- Maintain CI/CD health.
- Define user-impact-based SLOs.
- Ensure system reliability standards.

## Senior Developer (L4)

### Overview

- Primary activity: delivering software through direct contribution and team multiplication.
- Operate with deep system understanding, strong product awareness, and business-outcome focus.

### Responsibilities

- Technical Leadership:
- Design and deliver system-level solutions.
- Influence APIs and architecture decisions.
- Identify and prioritize tech debt, system improvements, and user-impacting work.
- AI & Development Efficiency:
- Integrate advanced AI tools into workflows.
- Create shared components, templates, and patterns.
- Improve systems AI depends on (APIs, docs, design systems).
- Ensure team understands engineering fundamentals.
- System Thinking:
- Understand service interactions and dependencies.
- Identify risks from coupling and system changes.
- Think beyond individual components.
- Product Thinking:
- Identify product/engineering tradeoffs others miss.
- Drive early validation practices in the team.
- Enforce closing the loop as a team standard.
- Understand product metrics, customer impact, and business outcomes (acquisition and retention).
- Quality:
- Define and uphold quality standards.
- Ensure strong error handling, clear documentation, and consistent API contracts.
- Maintain attention to detail across naming, data integrity, and user experience.
- Delivery & Ownership:
- Deliver at System level.
- Own team output end-to-end.
- Scope and lead initiatives.
- Maintain high shipping velocity.
- Team Impact:
- Multiply team effectiveness.
- Mentor Developers and Graduate Developers.
- Set standards for thoroughness, engineering discipline, and collaboration.
- Collaboration:
- Work with senior engineers across teams, technical leaders in group, and product stakeholders.
- Represent team in cross-org discussions.
- Communicate technical decisions and product outcomes.

### To Get To Principal Developer (L5)

- Continuously reassess assumptions and adapt.
- Influence all technical members across Go1.
- Build a strong engineering culture.
- Identify org-level risks and opportunities early.
- Drive decisions aligned with business outcomes.
- Be recognized as a long-term technical leader.
- Promote simplicity and system-wide consistency.
