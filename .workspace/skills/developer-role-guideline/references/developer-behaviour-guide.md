# Developer Behaviour Guide

Use this file to compare expected behaviour at L3 vs L4 for each competency item.

## Personal Growth

### Growth Mindset (pg1)

- Description: Actively learns, applies new knowledge, and expands abilities.
- Developer (L3): Learns from senior developers. Uses leading-edge tools to enhance efficiency.
- Senior Developer (L4): Continuously reassesses own assumptions. Mentors others to learn rather than lean.

### Feedback & Reflection (pg2)

- Description: Seeks, gives, and acts on constructive feedback.
- Developer (L3): Actively shares concerns when disagreeing but commits wholly when a decision is made.
- Senior Developer (L4): Values diversity of opinions and harmonises discordant views. Exhibits humility in technical excellence.

## Collaboration

### Stakeholder Engagement (col1)

- Description: Works effectively with partners, PMs, and stakeholders to define and deliver outcomes.
- Developer (L3): Collaborates with team, PMs, and TPMs. Communicates with other teams as needed.
- Senior Developer (L4): Works with Partner Teams, Sr Devs, Principal Devs. Represents team to other organisations.

### Mentoring & Multiplying (col2)

- Description: Uplifts others and makes the team more effective.
- Developer (L3): Sometimes mentors new hires and interns.
- Senior Developer (L4): Force multiplies for the team. Actively interviews. Finds ways to enable team to do more each day.

## Team Impact

### Team Process Contribution (ti1)

- Description: Actively participates in and improves team rituals and processes.
- Developer (L3): Contributes to stand-up, backlog review, and retrospectives. Delivers demos at organisation forums.
- Senior Developer (L4): Continuously improves developer experience and operational support. Drives team process improvements.

### Hiring Involvement (ti2)

- Description: Participates in recruiting and candidate assessment.
- Developer (L3): Interviews Graduate Developers and Developers.
- Senior Developer (L4): Interviews Graduate Developers, Developers, and Senior Developers.

## Dealing with Ambiguity

### Navigating Uncertainty (amb1)

- Description: Works effectively when requirements or solutions are unclear.
- Developer (L3): Requirements mostly defined; able to disambiguate solutions. Actively keeps self unblocked.
- Senior Developer (L4): Comfortable with ambiguity. Overcomes obstacles. Communicates plan changes clearly to the right stakeholders.

### Decision Making (amb2)

- Description: Makes and owns calls, escalates appropriately, and commits fully.
- Developer (L3): Disagrees and commits. Actively keeps self unblocked. Escalates early.
- Senior Developer (L4): Drives right technical decisions spanning team ownership. Considers team and business outcomes.

## Delivery

### Scope of Delivery (del1)

- Description: Delivers software at the appropriate level of complexity.
- Developer (L3): Delivers at App/Service level. Understands interactions between software and other teams.
- Senior Developer (L4): Delivers at System level. Understands interactions across multiple App/Services. Influences APIs and system-wide decisions.

### Technical Vision (del2)

- Description: Understands and contributes to roadmap and business goals.
- Developer (L3): Looks multiple sprints ahead. Understands how work maps to customer value.
- Senior Developer (L4): Has knowledge of 6+ month roadmap. Understands business trade-offs and long-term goals.

## Quality

### Code Quality & Testing (qual1)

- Description: Writes high-quality, well-tested, maintainable code.
- Developer (L3): Code reviews are uneventful. Writes unit, integration, and other tests. Builds for testability.
- Senior Developer (L4): Exemplary code quality. Defines quality standards with team. Creates automated quality solutions.

### Tech Debt & Legacy (qual2)

- Description: Proactively identifies and manages tech debt.
- Developer (L3): Revisits and improves legacy systems for better efficiency when needed.
- Senior Developer (L4): Creates plans for dealing with legacy code and tech debt. Encourages simplicity in architecture.

## Operations

### CI/CD & Pipelines (ops1)

- Description: Maintains and improves delivery pipelines.
- Developer (L3): Understands CI/CD pipeline and production operation. Implements appropriate metrics, logging, and alarming.
- Senior Developer (L4): Ensures CI/CD pipelines are correct. Sets availability and failure rate goals. Actively improves pipeline health.

### Security & Reliability (ops2)

- Description: Applies security best practices and ensures system reliability.
- Developer (L3): Understands and applies security best practices.
- Senior Developer (L4): Ensures security and auditing best practices across team output. Assesses and improves team infrastructure and security.

## AI/GenAI — Systems Thinking

### Business Context for AI (ai1)

- Description: Understands business goals and constraints to guide AI-driven development effectively.
- Developer (L3): Understands current system well enough to instruct AI tools on component-level tasks with correct context.
- Senior Developer (L4): Articulates current and future system state to guide AI across the system. Understands near and long-term business goals.

### Delta Identification (ai2)

- Description: Identifies the most efficient path from current to desired system state when guiding AI tools.
- Developer (L3): Identifies clear component-level changes needed to achieve desired outcomes.
- Senior Developer (L4): Identifies efficient system-level deltas and understands how AI-driven changes affect multiple services and teams.

## AI/GenAI — Attention to Detail

### AI Output Validation (ai3)

- Description: Critically reviews and validates AI-generated code for correctness, quality, and fitness for production.
- Developer (L3): Reads and understands every line of AI output. Validates correctness, approach, and customer problem fit.
- Senior Developer (L4): Validates AI output for correctness, scaling, usability, and performance. Ensures non-functionals are included.

### AI Practice Improvement (ai4)

- Description: Continuously improves how the team uses AI tools and shares learnings.
- Developer (L3): Documents and stores prompts and model details. Iterates to achieve higher quality with fewer cycles.
- Senior Developer (L4): Establishes quality standards for AI-assisted development across the team's output.
