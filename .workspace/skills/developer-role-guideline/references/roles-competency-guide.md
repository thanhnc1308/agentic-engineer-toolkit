# Roles Competency Guide

Source: `go1-dev-competency.html` (Role Guidelines / "To Get To" matrix).

Use this guide to score promotion-readiness criteria by current role.

## 1. Personal Growth

### Developer (L3) criteria

- I actively dedicate time to learning new technologies, tools, and techniques.
- I apply what I learn to improve my day-to-day work and share learnings with the team.
- Next-level expectation: You continue to learn and expand your abilities. You are looked to as someone who can be easily approached for help and encourage others to learn rather than lean.

### Senior Developer (L4) criteria

- I continuously learn and expand my abilities, regularly reassessing my own assumptions.
- I am approachable for help and encourage others to learn rather than lean on me.
- Next-level expectation: You continue to grow and learn. You continuously reassess your own assumptions to ensure your decisions are the best they can be every day.

## 2. Collaboration

### Developer (L3) criteria

- I understand how my software interacts with and impacts other teams' systems.
- I consider customer impact when making technical decisions and delivering software.
- Next-level expectation: You can work effectively with different stakeholders and partners to define solutions to complex, partially defined problems. You build trust with every interaction outside your team.

### Senior Developer (L4) criteria

- I work effectively with stakeholders and partners to define solutions to complex, partially defined problems.
- I build trust with every interaction outside my team.
- Next-level expectation: You positively influence every technical member of the Go1 team. You work with leaders across the company to align day-to-day actions to Go1 values.

## 3. Team Interaction

### Developer (L3) criteria

- I actively participate in and contribute to team ceremonies like stand-ups, retros, and backlog refinement.
- I contribute positively to team morale and help improve team processes.
- Next-level expectation: You continuously improve your team, from developer experience to operational support. You force multiply for your team. You are an active interviewer.

### Senior Developer (L4) criteria

- I continuously improve my team's developer experience and operational support.
- I force multiply for my team and actively participate in hiring and interviewing.
- Next-level expectation: Your actions help Go1 to become an organisation that people enjoy being a part of. You exhibit humility in your technical excellence.

## 4. Dealing with Ambiguity

### Developer (L3) criteria

- I complete and deliver my work independently without needing constant guidance.
- I proactively unblock myself when stuck, escalating early when appropriate.
- Next-level expectation: You are comfortable with ambiguity and can overcome obstacles and setbacks. You communicate changes to plans clearly, quickly and to the right stakeholders.

### Senior Developer (L4) criteria

- I am comfortable with ambiguity and can overcome obstacles and setbacks.
- I communicate changes to plans clearly, quickly, and to the right stakeholders.
- Next-level expectation: You actively identify organisation technology issues before they are problems. You identify potential innovations, championing them to senior leadership for sponsorship.

## 5. Delivery

### Developer (L3) criteria

- I consistently deliver quality software components on time and to the expected standard.
- My work meaningfully contributes to the team's overall output and goals.
- Next-level expectation: You consistently drive towards the right technical decisions spanning vertically through your team's ownership, considering both outcomes for the team and the business.

### Senior Developer (L4) criteria

- I consistently drive towards the right technical decisions spanning my team's ownership.
- I consider both team and business outcomes when making technical decisions.
- Next-level expectation: You demonstrate uplift of the Go1 customer experience through the implementation of your tech solutions across P&E, both through hands-on development and influencing other tech teams.

## 6. Quality

### Developer (L3) criteria

- My software is appropriately tested with unit, integration, and other relevant tests.
- My code reviews are uneventful — reviewers rarely find significant issues.
- Next-level expectation: Your code quality is exemplary. Your actions encourage automating quality rather than repetitive tasks.

### Senior Developer (L4) criteria

- My code quality is exemplary and sets the standard for my team.
- I actively encourage and implement automation of quality checks rather than relying on repetitive manual tasks.
- Next-level expectation: You are accepted as a technical leader within Go1 who brings perspectives to both problems and solutions that take a long-term view. You acknowledge that the cost of development is maintenance, not coding.

## 7. Operations

### Developer (L3) criteria

- I can identify, diagnose, and resolve component-level bugs without assistance.
- I create appropriate metrics, monitoring, logging, and alarming as part of software development.
- Next-level expectation: You keep the CI/CD pipelines in good health, adjusting them as needed. You actively set availability and failure rate goals.

### Senior Developer (L4) criteria

- I keep CI/CD pipelines in good health, adjusting them as needed.
- I actively set and monitor availability and failure rate goals for my team's services.
- Next-level expectation: You encourage simplicity in architecture and code, finding balance between over-engineered and incomplete, between improving legacy and building new.
