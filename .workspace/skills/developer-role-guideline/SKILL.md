# Developer Role Guideline Coach

Use this skill to evaluate Developer (L3) and Senior Developer (L4) capability using role baselines, competency criteria, and evidence examples.

## When To Use

- Calibrating a self-assessment or manager assessment for L3/L4 engineers
- Checking promotion readiness against "To Get To" role criteria
- Writing evidence-backed feedback, growth plans, or review notes
- Turning observed work into competency examples

## Core References

- Role overview and responsibilities (L3/L4): `references/role-overview-and-responsibilities.md`
- Role criteria guide: `references/roles-competency-guide.md`
- Behaviour expectations (L3 vs L4): `references/developer-behaviour-guide.md`
- Example evidence for L3: `references/competency-examples-l3.md`
- Example evidence for L4: `references/competency-examples-l4.md`

## Assessment Workflow

1. Confirm target role (`Developer (L3)` or `Senior Developer (L4)`) and scope period.
2. Calibrate against role baseline responsibilities in `references/role-overview-and-responsibilities.md`.
3. Score the 7 role criteria using `references/roles-competency-guide.md`.
4. Map observed behaviours to competency items in `references/developer-behaviour-guide.md`.
5. Use role-matched example catalogs to test evidence quality and identify gaps.
6. Produce a summary with strengths, risk areas, and next-step actions.

## Scoring Guidance (1-5)

- `1`: Not yet demonstrated
- `2`: Developing / inconsistent
- `3`: Applying with consistency
- `4`: Strong and repeatable
- `5`: Exemplary / force-multiplying

Use `3+` as the baseline threshold for readiness signals across role criteria.

## Guardrails

- Prefer observable outcomes over generic traits.
- Tie every score to specific evidence (PRs, incidents, docs, stakeholder outcomes).
- Distinguish one-off heroics from repeated behaviour.
- If evidence is missing, mark as "insufficient evidence" instead of assuming low performance.
